How to solve the 3x3 Rubik's Cube - Beginner Method

Cube notation

Before we learn how to solve the Rubik's Cube, you need to learn and understand something that will help you down the line a lot. That is cube notation. Cube notation is basically letters that we have given to each face of the cube which represents a turn. This is how we display algorithms. I will have some very basic and simple algorithms in this tutorial.
If you do not know anything about cube notation, here is the link that will explain it to you in more depth: Cube Notation

Step 1 - White Cross (white edges lining up on top)

The first step to solve the Rubik's Cube is to get a white cross. This step is basically getting all 4 edges matching with the center piece. In this tutorial I choose to make the white cross which means white will be the color I start with.
A lot of other tutorials recommend to do a daisy first (bringing the white edges to the yellow center), but I find that step unnecessary. Doing the white cross is very simple and only requires to learn a few moves. The goal here is to connect the white edge piece with the white center while making sure the 2nd color on the edge matches the center piece.

Here below I go over the most common cases for matching the white edge piece to the center piece. I only demonstrate with the white/green edge and white/red edge piece but you should do it with all 4. These common cases can be applied to any edge piece. Of course, they can be mirrored as well, which basically means you doing the same moves on the opposite side.

Step 2 - White Corners (Forming First Layer)

The second step consists of bringing the white corner pieces to their correct place in the correct position. This step is a lot easier than the first one because the corner piece can be divided in 3 main cases.
Before we go to the cases, I want to explain to you how you should approach this step. First of all, I want you to locate the white corner piece. Any white corner piece, pick one and locate it. Then, I want you to look at the other colors of the corner piece (corner piece = 3 colors). Lastly, I want you to check where that piece will go, in which correct spot. For example: I see a white corner piece with green and red colors, that means it should go in the corner spot between the colors white, green and red. Don't worry if you didn't fully understand that yet, it will get clear in the example cases below.

Step 3 - Middle Layer Edges

The third step will be making the middle layer by inserting the edges that don't have any top or bottom color centers (white or yellow in this case). To make this step easier, we will have to flip the cube upside down. This will cause the yellow center to be on top and the white center to be on the bottom.
The way this will work is that you will have to find an edge piece (middle piece with 2 colors) that has no yellow color on it. Then, You want the color facing you aligned with the correct center color. Look at the top color, that will indicate where the piece should go.
